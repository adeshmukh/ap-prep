# AP US History — Syllabus

Nine chronological periods defined by the College Board. Use these as quiz scope.

| Unit | Period     | Theme                                                      |
| ---- | ---------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | 1491–1607  | Pre-Columbian societies, contact, Columbian Exchange       |
| 2    | 1607–1754  | Colonial America, regional development, transatlantic trade |
| 3    | 1754–1800  | French & Indian War, Revolution, Constitution, early republic |
| 4    | 1800–1848  | Jeffersonian era, market revolution, Jacksonian democracy, reform |
| 5    | 1844–1877  | Manifest Destiny, sectionalism, Civil War, Reconstruction  |
| 6    | 1865–1898  | Gilded Age, industrialization, immigration, the West       |
| 7    | 1890–1945  | Progressive Era, WWI, 1920s, Great Depression, New Deal, WWII |
| 8    | 1945–1980  | Cold War, civil rights, Great Society, Vietnam, 1970s crises |
| 9    | 1980–present | Conservative resurgence, end of Cold War, globalization, post-2000 |

## Question types on the real exam

- **Multiple Choice** — stimulus-based (primary/secondary source with 3–4 linked questions).
- **Short-Answer Questions (SAQs)** — brief, structured responses.
- **Document-Based Question (DBQ)** — essay using 7 provided documents.
- **Long Essay Question (LEQ)** — thesis-driven essay without documents.

## Historical thinking skills / reasoning processes

Contextualization, comparison, causation, continuity and change over time (CCOT), sourcing, argumentation.
