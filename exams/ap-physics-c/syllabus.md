# AP Physics C — Syllabus

AP Physics C is two separate calculus-based exams: **Mechanics** and **Electricity & Magnetism**. Both are in scope here. When choosing a quiz unit, specify which subject (or use the unit number — they're unique across both).

Course framework: **2024 College Board CED** (effective 2024–25 school year). Units are numbered sequentially 1–13 across both subjects, per the official CED.

## Mechanics (Units 1–7)

| Unit | Title                                       | Exam Weighting |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| 1    | Kinematics                                  | 10–15%         |
| 2    | Force and Translational Dynamics            | 20–25%         |
| 3    | Work, Energy, and Power                     | 15–25%         |
| 4    | Linear Momentum                             | 10–20%         |
| 5    | Torque and Rotational Dynamics              | 10–15%         |
| 6    | Energy and Momentum of Rotating Systems     | 10–15%         |
| 7    | Oscillations                                | 10–15%         |

**Note on gravitation:** no longer a standalone unit. Gravitational force appears under Unit 2; orbital motion under Unit 6 (Motion of Orbiting Satellites).

## Electricity & Magnetism (Units 8–13)

| Unit | Title                                        | Exam Weighting |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------- | -------------- |
| 8    | Electric Charges, Fields, and Gauss's Law    | 15–25%         |
| 9    | Electric Potential                           | 10–20%         |
| 10   | Conductors and Capacitors                    | 10–15%         |
| 11   | Electric Circuits (includes RC circuits)     | 15–25%         |
| 12   | Magnetic Fields and Electromagnetism         | 10–20%         |
| 13   | Electromagnetic Induction (includes LR, LC)  | 10–20%         |

## Exam format (per subject)

- **Multiple Choice:** 40 questions, 4 options each (A–D), 80 minutes, 50% of score. Administered digitally.
- **Free Response:** 4 multi-part problems, 100 minutes, 50% of score. Handwritten.
- Calculator permitted throughout. Official equation/constants reference sheet provided.
- Mechanics and E&M are administered in **separate testing sessions**.

## Math expectations

Fluency with derivatives, integrals, vector operations, and differential equations of the form used in RC/LR/LC circuits and SHM.
